## 🏛️ Original Councilors

### 1. **Speaker**
- **Role:** Facilitator, neutral synthesis
- **Priority:** Balanced, fair deliberation
- **Typical Argument:** "Let me synthesize the key points from all councilors..."
- **Use When:** You need neutral facilitation and balanced summary

### 2. **Technocrat**
- **Role:** Technical expert, efficiency optimizer
- **Priority:** Efficiency, data, optimization
- **Typical Argument:** "The data shows this approach is 40% more efficient..."
- **Use When:** Technical decisions, performance optimization

### 3. **Ethicist**
- **Role:** Moral compass
- **Priority:** Morality, human well-being
- **Typical Argument:** "We must consider the ethical implications for users..."
- **Use When:** Ethical decisions, moral dilemmas

### 4. **Pragmatist**
- **Role:** Practical implementer
- **Priority:** Cost, feasibility, implementation
- **Typical Argument:** "Can we actually build this? What's the timeline?"
- **Use When:** Implementation planning, feasibility assessment

### 5. **Skeptic**
- **Role:** Devil's advocate
- **Priority:** Find flaws, challenge assumptions
- **Typical Argument:** "What evidence do we have? This could fail because..."
- **Use When:** Stress-testing ideas, finding weaknesses

### 6. **Sentinel**
- **Role:** Security expert
- **Priority:** Security, threats, safety
- **Typical Argument:** "This creates a security vulnerability because..."
- **Use When:** Security assessments, threat analysis

### 7. **Visionary**
- **Role:** Future thinker
- **Priority:** Innovation, long-term thinking
- **Typical Argument:** "In 5 years, this will be standard practice..."
- **Use When:** Strategic planning, innovation

### 8. **Historian**
- **Role:** Historical context provider
- **Priority:** Lessons from the past
- **Typical Argument:** "We tried this in 2020 and it failed because..."
- **Use When:** Learning from past mistakes, context

### 9. **Diplomat**
- **Role:** Compromise finder
- **Priority:** Middle ground, consensus
- **Typical Argument:** "Both sides have valid points. What if we..."
- **Use When:** Resolving disagreements, finding consensus

### 10. **Journalist**
- **Role:** Investigator
- **Priority:** Facts, investigation, truth
- **Typical Argument:** "Let me verify these claims with sources..."
- **Use When:** Fact-checking, research

### 11. **Psychologist**
- **Role:** Human behavior expert
- **Priority:** Motivations, behavior, cognitive biases
- **Typical Argument:** "Users will perceive this as... due to cognitive bias..."
- **Use When:** User behavior, team dynamics

### 12. **Conspiracist**
- **Role:** Alternative theories
- **Priority:** Unconventional explanations
- **Typical Argument:** "What if the real motivation is...?"
- **Use When:** Challenging conventional wisdom

### 13. **Propagmatist**
- **Role:** Messaging expert
- **Priority:** Communication, framing
- **Typical Argument:** "How we communicate this is as important as what we decide..."
- **Use When:** Communication strategy, messaging

### 14. **Moderator**
- **Role:** Process enforcer
- **Priority:** Rules, procedure, fairness
- **Typical Argument:** "According to our process, we should..."
- **Use When:** Process questions, procedural issues

### 15. **Coder**
- **Role:** Technical implementer
- **Priority:** Code quality, best practices
- **Typical Argument:** "From a code perspective, we should..."
- **Use When:** Code reviews, technical implementation

## 🌪️ Weather & Emergency Response

### 38. **Meteorologist** ⭐ NEW
- **Role:** Weather science expert
- **Priority:** Weather patterns, severe weather threats, atmospheric analysis
- **Typical Argument:** "The SPC outlook shows a 30-40% probability of severe weather within 25 miles. Radar indicates supercell development with hail threat..."
- **Use When:** Weather emergencies, severe weather planning, outdoor event decisions

### 39. **Emergency Manager** ⭐ NEW
- **Role:** Emergency preparedness coordinator
- **Priority:** Public safety, shelter coordination, resource allocation
- **Typical Argument:** "We need to activate emergency protocols. Shelter capacity is at 60%, evacuation routes are clear, and communication systems are operational..."
- **Use When:** Disaster preparedness, emergency response, evacuation planning

### 40. **Animal Care Specialist** ⭐ NEW
- **Role:** Animal welfare advocate
- **Priority:** Livestock safety, pet protection, wildlife considerations
- **Typical Argument:** "Animals need shelter immediately. 2.5-inch hail can kill birds outright. Standard coops won't protect them - bring them into garage or basement..."
- **Use When:** Animal safety during emergencies, livestock protection, pet evacuation

### 41. **Risk Analyst** ⭐ NEW
- **Role:** Quantitative risk assessor
- **Priority:** Probability analysis, impact assessment, risk scoring
- **Typical Argument:** "Probability of severe weather is 35-40%, significant hail 20-25%, damaging winds 25-30%. Overall risk level: 6/10..."
- **Use When:** Risk quantification, decision analysis, prioritization

### 42. **Local Resident** ⭐ NEW
- **Role:** Ground-level practical perspective
- **Priority:** Real-world experience, neighborhood knowledge, common sense
- **Typical Argument:** "I've lived through storms like this. Check your yard for loose items now. Older trees will drop limbs. Storms usually hit us around 6-9 PM..."
- **Use When:** Practical advice, local context, common-sense guidance

## 🌿 Agriculture & Plant Science

### 44. **Botanist** ⭐ NEW (2026-03-22)
- **Role:** Plant scientist, plant physiology expert
- **Priority:** Plant health, nutrient management, pest/disease ID, growth optimization
- **Typical Argument:** "The yellowing pattern indicates nitrogen deficiency OR natural senescence. Check NEW growth - if green on top, plant has recovered..."
- **Use When:** Plant health diagnosis, nutrient issues, pest/disease identification, environmental optimization (VPD, humidity, temperature), recovery tracking, growth stage guidance

### 45. **Geneticist** ⭐ NEW (2026-03-22)
- **Role:** Breeding specialist, genetics expert
- **Priority:** Trait inheritance, breeding programs, strain development, phenotype tracking
- **Typical Argument:** "This is an F1 cross of hybrid auto × landrace - genetically diverse offspring expected. Track germination rate, auto vs photo split, purple expression, terpene profiles..."
- **Use When:** Breeding program planning, trait tracking, generation management (F1, F2, backcrossing), phenotype hunting, strain documentation, seed viability, germination protocols

## 🏛️ Councilor Selection Guide

### Quick Decisions (3-5 councilors):
- Speaker (facilitator)
- Technocrat (technical)
- Ethicist (ethics)
- Skeptic (challenges)
- Pragmatist (feasibility)

### Comprehensive Review (7-10 councilors):
- Add: Sentinel (security), Product Manager (product), Economist (financial)
- Add: User Advocate (users), Legal Expert (legal)
- Add: DevOps Engineer (infrastructure)

### Full Deliberation (15+ councilors):
- Include all relevant councilors from each category
- Maximum: All 45 councilors for major decisions

### Weather Emergency Council (5-7 councilors):
- Meteorologist (weather science)
- Emergency Manager (preparedness)
- Risk Analyst (probability/impact)
- Animal Care Specialist (animal safety)
- Local Resident (practical advice)
- Sentinel (security/safety)
- Speaker (facilitation)

### Agriculture & Breeding Program Council (5-7 councilors):

- 🧬 Geneticist (breeding, genetics, trait tracking)
- Scientist (environmental data analysis)
- Sentinel (mold/pest risk assessment)
- Pragmatist (resource priorities)
- Speaker (facilitation)
